# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Guest-house and small-business owners on the Garden Route (South Africa) who received a WhatsApp/email from Elysium Labs and are clicking through to answer one question: "is this outfit legit and good?" They are non-technical, mostly on phones, often mid-workday. Secondary: larger prospective clients sizing up the studio's ambition.

## Product Purpose

The public face of Elysium Labs, a one-person digital studio that designs and builds websites, landing pages, games and software end to end — across hospitality, breweries, the arts and beyond. The site's job is to convert outreach-lead curiosity into trust within 30 seconds — and its own craft is the pitch: "the site you're looking at is the product." A curated Work section (a few real builds, professionally presented — never a dump) carries the proof. The visitor's reaction should be "wow — this guy knows what he's doing," achieved through craft and one theatrical gesture, not busyness. Success = a lead who lands here replies to the outreach message.

## Brand Personality

Precise, monumental, quietly confident. "We build things." — plain words, exceptional execution. The brand is the Architect's Mark: pure white space, ink-black type, one electric blue (#0000F2) reserved for the word "labs", and the 3D Monumental-E sculpture as the single theatrical gesture. Calm surface, deep craft.

## Anti-references

- Neon/cyberpunk aesthetics (that's Luke's internal tooling style, never this brand)
- Template agency sites: icon-card grids, stock team photos, "we're passionate about pixels" copy
- Editorial-magazine affectation (italic serif everywhere, drop caps, broadsheet grids)
- Dark-mode-by-default tech portfolio look; this brand is white
- Anything that reads busier than the client sites it sells

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the portfolio.** Every scroll interaction, every load time, every detail is a live demo of what a client is buying.
2. **Show receipts.** Real client work (guest-house builds) beats claims; proof over promises.
3. **One theatrical gesture, everything else calm.** The Monumental-E carries the drama; the rest of the page stays inked, white and typographic.
4. **Mobile is the first screen, not an adaptation.** Leads open this from WhatsApp on a phone; 360px is the primary canvas.
5. **Plain speech, exact craft.** Copy stays short and human ("We build things."); the sophistication lives in execution, not adjectives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against the white field (mind the text-over-sculpture halo). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` fallbacks for the 3D scene, parallax and reveals — the page must be complete with all motion off. Touch targets ≥44px. The 3D canvas is decorative (`aria-hidden`) and the page must read perfectly without WebGL.
